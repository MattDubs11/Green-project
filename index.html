Montefiore Square Reimagined

A Harlem-inspired wellness and community plaza redesign

About the Project

This proposal redesigns Montefiore Square into a safer, greener, and more community-centered urban plaza. The core goal is to improve pedestrian comfort, public life, and neighborhood identity through better seating, planting, circulation, and programming.

The Problem

Safety

Low visibility, undefined crossings, and weak nighttime lighting reduce comfort.

Comfort

Limited shade, rigid seating, and lack of seasonal planting make the plaza less inviting.

Community Use

The site currently lacks flexible zones for markets, events, and local gathering.

Site / Context

Site analysis focused on pedestrian movement, accessibility, and neighborhood business activity. The redesign prioritizes circulation loops, rain gardens, ADA seating edges, and stronger storefront connections.

Design Options

Option A: Wellness garden plaza

Option B: Weekend vendor plaza

Option C: Cultural art and seating plaza

Final Proposal

The final concept combines Harlem cultural identity, green infrastructure, curved social seating, better lighting, and vendor-ready flexible programming space.

Reflection on AI Use

AI supported fast visual exploration and helped compare multiple design atmospheres. Human judgment was essential for choosing realistic circulation, accessibility, and community-based programming decisions.
