Montefiore Square Reimagined

A greener Harlem plaza with wellness, seating, and community life

About the Project

This redesign transforms Montefiore Square into a greener, pedestrian-centered public plaza focused on shade, planting, seating, and neighborhood gathering.

The Problem

Safety

Poor lighting and undefined pedestrian crossings reduce comfort.

Comfort

Too much hardscape and not enough shade or flexible seating.

Community

The plaza lacks spaces for vendors, events, and wellness activity.

Site / Context

The redesign improves circulation, ADA accessibility, rain gardens, and storefront connections while strengthening Harlem neighborhood identity.

Design Options

Option A: Green wellness plaza

Option B: Community market plaza

Option C: Safer lighting plaza

Final Proposal

The final proposal combines lush planting, curved social seating, improved pedestrian flow, and a welcoming Harlem-centered atmosphere.

Reflection on AI Use

AI helped rapidly explore multiple green-plaza concepts, while human design judgment guided circulation, realism, and community programming choices.
